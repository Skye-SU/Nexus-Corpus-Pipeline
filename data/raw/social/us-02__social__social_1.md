Bluesky post arguing that the Copyright Office's human-authorship requirement compels corporations to employ human creators.

## Raw Text

John Linnell (@linnell.bsky.social):

“the Copyright Office has maintained – correctly – that AI-generated works cannot be copyrighted, because copyright is exclusively for humans…[this] means that the only way [Getty or Disney or Universal or Hearst] can get a copyright is to pay humans to do creative work.”
